# /book-meeting

**Usage:**
- `/book-meeting [name] [meeting type]`
- `/book-meeting Sarah Jones discovery call`
- `/book-meeting James from Acme demo this week`

**What it does:** Checks the real calendar for availability,
proposes 3 times, drafts the email, sends on approval, creates
the event, sends the invite, and updates the CRM.
Full end-to-end. No manual steps.

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Identify the contact
- Search CRM for the name or company provided
- Pull their email address, what business they are acquiring if known,
  and any relevant context from previous interactions
- Note their timezone if mentioned anywhere — default to GMT if unknown

### Step 2 — Determine meeting type and duration

Discovery call — 20 minutes
Use when: they are curious about Bizzed but have no live deal yet
Goal: understand their situation and book a demo

Product demo — 30 minutes
Use when: they have a specific business they are looking to acquire
Goal: show Bizzed on their actual target, move to platform signup

Financing consultation — 45 minutes
Use when: they have completed analysis and want to explore funding
Goal: understand their funding gap and connect to financing options

Partnership or press call — 30 minutes
Use when: they are a broker, advisor, or journalist
Goal: explore referral partnership or press opportunity

### Step 3 — Check the real calendar
- Open Google Calendar
- Find 3 genuine available slots respecting all scheduling rules
- Spread across at least 2 different days
- Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons where possible

### Step 4 — Draft the scheduling email

Keep the whole email under 80 words.
Reference their specific situation — do not send a generic booking email.

For a demo booking, include this line:
"To make the demo as useful as possible, if you can share the website URL
or any financials for the business you're looking at beforehand, I'll have
it loaded up and ready to go."

Show the draft for approval before sending.

### Step 5 — On approval, send and book
1. Send the email via Gmail
2. Create a provisional calendar event (mark as tentative)
3. Update CRM: add note "Meeting proposed — awaiting confirmation"
   and set a 48-hour follow-up reminder if no reply

### Step 6 — When they confirm a time
1. Confirm the calendar event — change from tentative to confirmed
2. Add a Google Meet link
3. Send a formal calendar invite with a brief agenda in the description
4. If it is a demo: note in the event what business they are acquiring
   and whether they have shared any financials
5. Update CRM: log confirmed meeting date, move deal stage
6. Set a meeting prep reminder for 30 minutes before the meeting
7. Confirm: "Done — [meeting type] with [name] confirmed for
   [date] at [time]. Invite sent."
