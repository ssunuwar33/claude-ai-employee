# /follow-up

**Usage:**
- `/follow-up [name]` — pulls full history from CRM automatically
- `/follow-up [name] [extra context]` — uses provided context

**What it does:** Checks the full history with this contact,
works out which follow-up number this is, writes a message that
doesn't sound like a follow-up, sends on approval, and logs
the follow-up number in CRM.

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Pull the history from CRM
- Last contact date and how many days ago that was
- What was discussed or sent
- What stage they are at
- What their acquisition target is
- Any objections or concerns they raised previously
- How many follow-ups have already been sent

### Step 2 — Determine which follow-up this is

First follow-up (3 days after last contact):
Warm and helpful. Add one new piece of value.
Options: a relevant insight about their target market,
a new Bizzed capability, a proof point, a question about their deal.

Second follow-up (7 days after last contact):
Brief and direct. Acknowledge they are busy.
Ask one simple yes or no question to get a response.

Third follow-up — closing email (14+ days with no response):
Short. Respectful. Remove all pressure.
Close the loop without burning the relationship.

### Step 3 — Write the follow-up

Rules that apply to all follow-ups:
- Never open with "Just following up", "Checking in", or "Circling back"
- Always reference something specific — their deal, their target, their timeline
- Keep under 100 words — shorter is better
- End with one soft specific ask

First follow-up structure:
[One sentence referencing their specific acquisition target or situation]
+ [One new thing — insight, stat, capability, or relevant question]
+ [Soft ask — e.g. "Worth a 20-minute call this week?"]

Second follow-up structure:
[Acknowledge the busy nature of deal-making]
+ [One line on what is at stake for their specific deal]
+ [Simple yes or no question — e.g. "Still looking at the [business name] deal?"]

Third follow-up — closing email:
"Hi [Name] — I'll assume the timing isn't right for Bizzed right now
and I'll stop nudging you.

If you come back to the [target business] acquisition or start looking
at another deal, we'll be here — analysis is always free to get started.

Wishing you well with whatever you're working on."

### Step 4 — Show for approval then send
1. Send via Gmail on approval
2. Update CRM: log follow-up number and date
3. If first or second: set the next follow-up reminder
4. If third — closing email: move deal to Closed Lost — No Response in CRM.
   Add a note explaining timeline and what was tried.
   Do not delete the record — they may return when a new deal starts.
