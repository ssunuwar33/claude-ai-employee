# /morning-brief

**What it does:** Pulls everything from email, calendar, and CRM
to give a complete prioritised picture of the day.
Run this every morning. Replaces 45 minutes of manual checking.

---

## Instructions

Execute these steps in order. Do not skip any.

### Step 1 — Check the calendar
- Pull today's full calendar
- For each meeting note: time, who it is with, what type of meeting,
  and what preparation is needed
- Flag any meeting that has no context or notes attached

### Step 2 — Scan emails
- Check all unread emails received since yesterday at 6pm
- Categorise each one as: Urgent / Reply needed / FYI / Ignore
- For Urgent and Reply needed: summarise what they want and
  what action is needed from Bizzed
- Do not reply to anything yet — just report what needs attention

### Step 3 — Check the CRM
- Pull all leads and deals that have had activity in the last 48 hours
- Flag any hot leads that have not been contacted yet today
- Flag any deals where the next action is overdue
- Flag anyone who signed up to the waitlist in the last 24 hours

### Step 4 — Produce the brief

Output in this exact format — nothing more, nothing less:

---
BIZZED — MORNING BRIEF
[Day, Date]

TODAY'S MEETINGS
[Time] — [Who] — [Meeting type] — [Prep needed: yes/no + what]

EMAILS NEEDING ACTION
[Sender] — [What they need] — [Suggested action]

CRM ALERTS
[Name/Company] — [Stage] — [Issue or overdue action]

NEW WAITLIST SIGNUPS
[Name] — [Any context from their signup]

TOP 3 PRIORITIES TODAY
1. [Most important thing]
2. [Second most important]
3. [Third]

ANYTHING ELSE TO KNOW
[Anything that doesn't fit above but matters today]
---

Keep every single line under 20 words.
No waffle. No filler. The whole brief should be readable in 90 seconds.
