# /qualify-lead

**Usage:**
- `/qualify-lead` — pulls the most recent unqualified lead from CRM
- `/qualify-lead [name or company]` — qualifies a specific lead by name
- `/qualify-lead [paste their message or email]` — qualifies from raw text

**What it does:** Reads the lead's details, scores them using the
Bizzed qualification framework, produces a full report, then asks
which action to take and executes it immediately on confirmation.

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Gather the data

If pulling from CRM:
- Find the lead record
- Read: name, role, company, source, any notes, email thread if linked,
  what business they are looking to acquire if mentioned

If from pasted text or email:
- Extract all available information
- Note clearly what is missing and flag it in the report

### Step 2 — Score the lead
Apply the full 5-dimension scoring from the lead-qualification skill.
Be honest. Do not inflate scores.
Pay special attention to whether they have a live deal in progress —
this is the single biggest indicator of a hot lead for Bizzed.

### Step 3 — Produce the qualification report
Use the exact report format from the lead-qualification skill file.

### Step 4 — Recommend and offer to act

After the report, ask:

"What would you like me to do next?

A) Draft and send a personalised outreach email to them now
B) Check the calendar and propose 3 times for a discovery call
C) Check the calendar and propose 3 times for a product demo
   (use this if they already have a live acquisition target)
D) Add them to the waitlist nurture sequence and set a 30-day reminder
E) Log as poor fit and archive the record

Reply with A, B, C, D, or E."

### Step 5 — Execute the chosen action

A — Draft the outreach email using tone-of-voice skill.
    Reference their specific acquisition situation.
    Show for approval. Send on confirmation. Log in CRM.

B — Check Google Calendar for 3 real available slots.
    Draft the scheduling email. Show for approval.
    Send on confirmation. Set CRM next action.

C — Same as B but frame the meeting as a product demo.
    In the email, ask them to share the website URL or any
    financials for the business they are looking at —
    so the demo can be run on their actual target.

D — Send a warm welcome email acknowledging their interest.
    Invite them to the waitlist if not already on it.
    Add a 30-day follow-up task in CRM.

E — Update CRM status to Disqualified.
    Add a note explaining why.
    Archive the record.

### Step 6 — Log everything in CRM
Whatever action is taken:
- Action taken and date
- Lead classification and score
- Next step and due date
