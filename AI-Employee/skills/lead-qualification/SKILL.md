# Lead Qualification Framework

When asked to qualify a lead — from an email, form submission,
waitlist signup, or CRM entry — use this framework every time.
Score honestly. Do not inflate to look good.

---

## The 5 scoring dimensions (0 to 3 each, max 15)

---

### 1. Budget / Deal size

3 — They are actively looking at a business acquisition with a
    confirmed budget or financing in mind
2 — They are interested in acquiring but haven't confirmed budget yet
1 — Early stage, just exploring — no budget discussed
0 — No acquisition intent, or looking for something Bizzed can't help with

---

### 2. Authority

3 — They are the buyer making the decision themselves
2 — They are advising or supporting the actual buyer
1 — They are researching on behalf of someone else
0 — Unknown, or clearly not involved in any acquisition decision

---

### 3. Need

3 — Actively in a live deal or close to one — needs due diligence now
2 — Has a target in mind but not yet in active due diligence
1 — Exploring acquisitions generally, no specific target yet
0 — No clear acquisition need — may be curious or doing research

---

### 4. Timeline

3 — Looking to complete due diligence within the next 30 days
2 — Deal likely to progress within 1 to 3 months
1 — Longer term — 3 to 6 months out
0 — No timeline, or just browsing

---

### 5. ICP Fit

3 — Business buyer, entrepreneur, or investor actively acquiring SMEs
2 — Adjacent — advisor, broker, or accountant referring buyers to us
1 — Interested but not a direct buyer
0 — No fit — wrong use case entirely

---

## Scoring and recommended action

| Score   | Classification | Action |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| 12–15   | Hot           | Follow up within 2 hours. Get them onto the platform or a call. |
| 8–11    | Warm          | Send personalised email today. Follow up in 3 days. |
| 4–7     | Cold          | Add to nurture. Invite to waitlist. Revisit in 30 days. |
| 0–3     | Poor fit      | Politely decline. Refer if possible. Do not invest time. |

## Disqualify immediately if

- They are not involved in any business acquisition — wrong use case
- They are a competitor doing research
- They want a service Bizzed does not offer (e.g. property due diligence,
  investment fund analysis, personal financial advice)
- They have been flagged as a problem contact previously
