# Sales Process

This is the exact process for converting a Bizzed lead from
first contact to active platform user and financing customer.
Follow every stage in order. Never skip steps.

---

## Stage 1 — New lead or waitlist signup arrives

Trigger: waitlist signup, contact form, email, referral, or CRM entry
Time target: respond within 2 hours during business hours

What to do:
1. Qualify using the lead-qualification framework
2. If Hot — they have a live deal — contact within 2 hours.
   Goal: get them onto the platform or book a demo immediately.
3. If Warm — exploring acquisitions — send personalised email today.
   Reference their specific situation. Book a discovery call.
4. If Cold — early stage or just curious — send a welcome email.
   Invite them to join the waitlist if not already on it.
   Add a 30-day follow-up reminder.
5. If Poor Fit — politely decline or refer elsewhere.
6. Log everything in the CRM same day with a specific note.

---

## Stage 2 — Discovery call

Goal: understand exactly where they are in their acquisition journey.
Do not pitch features. Ask questions and listen.

Questions to always ask:
- "Have you found a specific business you're looking at, or are you
  still in the search phase?"
- "What does your current due diligence process look like?"
- "What's the biggest challenge for you right now in evaluating this deal?"
- "What's your timeline — are you under any pressure to move quickly?"
- "Have you looked at financing yet or is that a later conversation?"

After the call — within 1 hour:
1. Log full notes in CRM — specific, not vague
2. Send a follow-up email summarising what was discussed
3. Either: send them a platform access link to start their free analysis,
   or book a product demo for within 48 hours
4. Move deal stage in CRM

---

## Stage 3 — Product demo

Goal: show Bizzed working on their actual target business.
Not a generic demo. Use their real URL or their real P&L if shared.

Before the demo:
- Review their CRM notes
- Know the target business they are looking at
- Prepare the platform with their target loaded if possible

During the demo — show in this order:
1. Instant Insights from a website URL — show speed
2. Financial analysis from a P&L — show depth
3. Risk assessment output — show what they would have missed manually
4. Legal document generation — show completeness
5. Financing options — introduce how Bizzed helps them complete the deal

After the demo — within 2 hours:
1. Send a follow-up email with a direct link to start their free analysis
2. Log demo outcome in CRM
3. If they are ready: move to financing conversation
4. If they need time: set a 3-day follow-up

---

## Stage 4 — Objection handling

"Is my data safe?"
Say: "Bizzed is ISO 27001 certified, GDPR compliant, and uses
bank-level encryption. Every user's data is strictly isolated —
no one else sees your deal. Security is non-negotiable for us."

"How much does it cost?"
Say: "Analysing a business on Bizzed is completely free. We make
money by helping you access financing if you decide to move forward.
Our incentives are fully aligned — we only win when you complete your deal."

"Is the platform live yet?"
Say: "We're currently in early access after a successful Q1 2025 pilot.
The full launch is Q3 2025. I can get you priority access — you'd be
among the first in when we open up."

"How is this better than hiring an accountant?"
Say: "A traditional due diligence process takes weeks and costs thousands
before you've even decided if the deal is worth pursuing. Bizzed gives you
a complete picture in minutes, for free, so you can make that decision
with confidence — and bring in advisors only when it actually makes sense."

"What if I'm not technical?"
Say: "You don't need to be. Start with a website URL — that's it.
Bizzed does the analysis. You read the results. No spreadsheets,
no jargon, no financial background required."

---

## Stage 5 — Converting to financing

Trigger: user has completed their free analysis and is serious about the deal

What to do:
1. Review their analysis results with them
2. Understand their funding gap — how much they need to acquire the business
3. Present the financing options Bizzed can access for their situation
4. Connect them with the right financing partner
5. Log deal value in CRM — this is where Bizzed generates revenue
6. Stay close throughout — check in weekly until financing is confirmed

---

## CRM hygiene — non-negotiable

- Every interaction logged same day — no exceptions
- Notes must be specific: not "spoke to lead" but "20-min call with
  James, looking to acquire a Birmingham-based e-commerce business,
  asking price £400k, needs financing of £280k, wants a demo Thursday"
- Every open deal must have a next action and a due date
- Deal stage must always reflect reality — never log optimistically
- Never leave a deal with no next step scheduled
