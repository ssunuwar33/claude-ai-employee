# Scheduling and Calendar Management

When asked to check availability, propose times, or book a meeting —
always check the real calendar first. Never guess or assume availability.

---

## Working hours

Monday to Friday: 9:00am to 6:00pm GMT/BST
No external meetings before 9:30am
No meetings after 5:30pm
All times default to GMT unless the contact is in a different timezone —
if so, confirm their timezone and show times in both

---

## Meeting types and durations for Bizzed

Product demo / walkthrough — 30 minutes
For prospects who want to see Bizzed on their actual acquisition target.
This is the primary conversion meeting.

Discovery call — 20 minutes
For leads who are curious but haven't committed to a demo yet.
Goal: understand their situation and book a demo.

Financing consultation — 45 minutes
For users who have completed analysis and want to explore funding options.

Partnership or press call — 30 minutes
For brokers, advisors, or media inquiries.

---

## Buffer rules

Leave at least 30 minutes between consecutive external meetings.
Leave 15 minutes after every demo to write up notes and follow up.
Never book two demos back to back.

---

## How to propose times

Always offer exactly 3 options across at least 2 different days.
Format as a numbered list so they can reply with just a number.

Example:
I have a few slots this week:

1. Tuesday [date] at 10:30am GMT — 30 minutes
2. Wednesday [date] at 2:00pm GMT — 30 minutes
3. Thursday [date] at 11:00am GMT — 30 minutes

Which works best for you?

---

## When a meeting is confirmed

1. Create the calendar event with correct title, time, duration, attendees
2. Add a Google Meet or Zoom link
3. Send a calendar invite to all attendees
4. Add context to the event description:
   who they are, what business they are acquiring, what they want to see
5. Update the CRM — log confirmed meeting, move deal stage
6. Confirm to the team: "Done — [meeting type] booked with [name]
   on [date] at [time]. Invite sent."

---

## Pre-meeting brief format

WHO THEY ARE
[Name, role, how they found Bizzed]

THEIR ACQUISITION TARGET
[What business they are looking to acquire — industry, size, location if known]

THEIR SITUATION
[Where they are in the process — early research, live deal, post-offer]

GOAL FOR THIS MEETING
[What a successful outcome looks like — e.g. they start a free analysis,
they move to financing conversation, they refer a colleague]

KEY TALKING POINTS
[3 specific things to cover based on their situation]
