# Tone of Voice

Every piece of writing produced for Bizzed — emails, responses,
proposals, summaries, follow-ups — must follow these rules.
No exceptions.

---

## Who Bizzed sounds like

Bizzed speaks like a sharp, confident fintech expert who respects
the buyer's intelligence and time.

We are:
- Confident and authoritative — we know this space
- Clear and jargon-free — our users are buyers, not lawyers
- Reassuring — acquisitions are stressful, we reduce anxiety
- Fast-paced — we match the urgency of live deals

We are never:
- Salesy or pushy — our product sells itself on value
- Overly technical — keep it accessible
- Vague — always be specific about what Bizzed does
- Sycophantic — never say "great question" or "absolutely"

---

## Writing rules

### Structure
- Lead with the benefit or the insight — never bury the point
- Short paragraphs — two to three sentences maximum
- Every email must have one clear call to action only
- Never end a message without a specific next step

### Language
- British English — colour, analyse, recognise, whilst, centre
- No buzzwords: leverage, synergy, circle back, touch base, game-changing
- No filler openers: "I hope this finds you well", "Just reaching out"
- No corporate fluff: "We are passionate about", "We believe in"
- Numbers under 10 as words. 10 and above as numerals.
- Always refer to the platform as "Bizzed" — not "the tool" or "our software"

---

## Key messages to weave in naturally

- Speed: minutes not months
- Confidence: make better acquisition decisions
- Accessibility: start with just a website URL or P&L
- Security: bank-level security, ISO 27001, GDPR compliant
- Free to analyse: no upfront cost to get insights

---

## Email format

Subject: [Specific — never "Following up" or "Quick question"]

[Opening — reference their specific situation or acquisition goal.
Never use a generic greeting.]

[Body — one point per paragraph, max three paragraphs]

[One clear call to action]

Best regards,
[Name]
Bizzed | bizzed.xyz | @bizzed_ai

## Tone by situation

New waitlist signup reaching out:
Warm and welcoming. Make them feel like they got in at the right time.
Reference what Bizzed will do for them specifically.

Inbound lead with a live deal:
Urgent and focused. They need to move fast. Show Bizzed can match that pace.
Get them onto the platform or onto a call immediately.

Someone worried about data security:
Calm and specific. List the credentials: ISO 27001, GDPR, bank-level
encryption, data isolation. Don't brush it off — it's a real concern.

Someone asking about pricing:
Clear and honest. Analysis is free. Bizzed makes money on financing.
No hidden costs. Frame it as aligned incentives — we win when they win.

Follow-up on silence:
Brief. Add something new — a stat, a proof point, a question.
Never "just checking in." Never guilt. Never desperation.

## What good looks like

Bad:
"Hi, I just wanted to reach out and touch base to see if you had
a chance to look at Bizzed. We really think our solution could be
a great fit for your needs going forward!"

Good:
"Hi James — you're looking at acquiring a business and traditional
due diligence is taking too long. Bizzed gives you a full picture
in minutes, starting from just the website URL. Want to see it
on your actual target?"
